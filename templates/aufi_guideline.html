Ask: Does the patient have any red flag sign

Red Flag Criteria:

  • Hyperpyrexia (≥106.7°F), Hypothermia (≤95°F)
  • Prostration, Shortness of breath
  • Arterial oxygen saturation < 90%, BP < 90/<60 mm Hg
  • Seizure, altered sensorium, neck stiffness
  • Repeated vomiting or inability to tolerate oral intake
  • Jaundice, Bleeding

suggest admission

Ask: For how many days patient is suffering from fever

Care Guideline:

Consider supportive care without antibiotics

Result of: Test for dengue NS1 ELISA+ Peripheral blood smear (+ RDT) for malaria+ CBC

Treat accordingly dengue/ malaria based on positive result

Consider alternative diagnosis

List of test to be done

  • Dengue NS1 ELISA/IgM depending on duration of fever
  • Peripheral blood smear (+ RDT) for malaria if not done earlier
  • CBC
  • IgM Chikungunya
  • IgM Scrub Typhus
  • IgM Leptospirosis
  • Typhi dot & send blood culture for detecting typhoid
  • Chest X-Ray if respiratory symptoms present

Treatment Guidance Recommendations

Suspecting Typhoid?

fever, bradycardia, constipation/diarrhea, patient may present with toxic look

Symptoms may vary from above classical picture

If Typhoid is suspected: Azithromycin 1gm OD for 7 days, Avoid Fluroquinolones due to high resistance

Suspecting Scrub Typhus?

High fever, myalgia, dry cough, lymphadenopathy

Symptoms may vary from above classical picture

If Scrub Typhus is suspected: Doxycycline 100 mg BD for 7 days

Suspecting Leptospirosis?

Conjunctival suffusion, yellow eyes, myalgia

Symptoms may vary from above classical picture

Doxycycline 100 mg BD for 7 days

Suspecting Chikungunya

Sudden fever, polyarthralgia,

Early onset of rashes

Symptoms may vary from above classical picture

If Chikungunya suspected: Supportive care
